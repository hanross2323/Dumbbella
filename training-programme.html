Programme Philosophy & Periodisation

This programme uses Linear-to-Undulating Block Periodisation — the most effective evidence-based model for an intermediate natural athlete whose primary goal is hypertrophy with concurrent strength development. Rather than changing rep ranges randomly, we deliberately manipulate volume, intensity, and exercise variation across three structured 4-week blocks.

Why this model for you At 45, testosterone and recovery capacity are meaningfully different from a 25-year-old. This means we need to be smarter about fatigue management. Block periodisation lets us accumulate volume during accumulation phases and then "cash in" that adaptation during intensification phases — with strategic deloads to allow full supercompensation. You will make faster, more durable progress than with a generic programme.

Training Split Rationale

With 3 days and 45-minute sessions, a Push / Pull / Legs (PPL) split run on a rotating basis is optimal. Each muscle group is trained with sufficient volume (~10–14 sets/week) while allowing 48–72 hours recovery between sessions — critical for a 45-year-old natural athlete. This also means each workout has a clear primary stimulus with no overlap in fatigue patterns.

Weekly Schedule

Train on non-consecutive days where possible. Example: Monday (Push) · Wednesday (Pull) · Friday (Legs). The sessions rotate — Week 1 starts Push, Week 2 the first session of the week is Pull, etc. This ensures all sessions receive equal frequency across the programme.

Week-by-Week Progressive Overload Strategy

Progressive overload is the non-negotiable driver of adaptation. With fixed dumbbell weights (no microplates), we use rep progression and tempo manipulation first, then load jumps when rep targets are consistently exceeded. The hierarchy is: Add reps → Add sets → Slow tempo → Increase load.

The Double Progression Rule

On any given exercise, once you can complete the top end of the rep range for ALL sets with good form and leave 2+ reps in the tank, you increase the weight at the next session. Example: If the target is 3×10–12 and you hit 12, 12, 11 — move up next session. If you hit 10, 9, 8 — stay at this weight and add reps first.

Tempo Guide

Tempo is written as Eccentric – Pause at bottom – Concentric – Pause at top. "X" = explosive. Example: 3-1-2-0 means 3 seconds lowering, 1 second pause, 2 seconds lifting, 0 pause at top.

Phase 1 — Foundation

Slower eccentrics (3–4 sec) to maximise time under tension with lighter loads. This makes 12 kg feel genuinely challenging.

Phase 3 — Intensification

Explosive concentrics (X) on power movements (push press, step-up) to develop rate of force development — the neural strength adaptation.

04
Deload Protocol & Recovery Guidelines

When to Deload

Planned deloads occur at Week 4 and Week 8. A third deload is recommended after Week 12 before beginning a new training cycle. Additionally, apply an unplanned deload any week where 2+ of the following apply:

  • You feel unusually tired going into sessions for 3+ days
  • Performance is declining (less reps or load vs previous week) for 2 consecutive sessions
  • Joints feel sore rather than muscles (especially shoulders, knees, elbows)
  • Sleep quality has deteriorated noticeably
  • You have high life stress (work, travel, illness)

Why deloads matter even more at 45 Research shows that natural testosterone production is meaningfully lower at 45 than at 25. This extends recovery time between hard training blocks. Skipping deloads leads to accumulated fatigue that masks fitness gains — you don't feel or perform better even as adaptation is occurring. A proper deload week allows "supercompensation" — you come back stronger than before the deload, not just recovered to baseline.

Nutrition & Lifestyle Anchors

Training is the stimulus. The following are the conditions that allow adaptation to actually occur. These are not optional add-ons — they are co-determinants of your results.

Protein Target

At 92 kg, aim for 160–185 g protein per day (1.75–2.0 g/kg). This is the single most evidence-backed nutritional intervention for natural muscle building. Distribute across 3–4 meals. Leucine threshold per meal: ~30–40 g protein per sitting.

Calorie Strategy

For hypertrophy: a modest surplus of 200–300 kcal above maintenance. For your stats, estimated maintenance is ~2,400–2,600 kcal. Aim for 2,600–2,900 kcal on training days. Do not aggressive bulk — at 45, excess calories preferentially go to fat, not muscle.

Sleep

The single most anabolic free intervention available. 7–9 hours per night. Most testosterone release occurs during slow-wave sleep. Chronically sleeping under 6 hours reduces testosterone by up to 15% and doubles injury risk.

Recovery Between Sessions

Minimum 48 hours between sessions training the same muscle group. Your 3-day split naturally provides this. On rest days: walk, stretch, or swim. Avoid complete inactivity — light movement improves blood flow and recovery speed.

06
Post-Programme: What Comes Next

After completing Week 12, take a full deload week. Then reassess:

  • Test your heaviest dumbbell for 10 reps on each primary lift — record these as new baselines
  • If you've outgrown 12 kg dumbbells (likely on most exercises), invest in heavier DBs (16–20 kg range)
  • Run this programme again with Phase 1 starting loads set 10–15% higher than your original Week 1
  • After a second cycle, consider adding resistance bands or a pull-up bar to expand exercise variety
  • Reassess goals — by cycle 2, strength goals may warrant barbell investment
Coach's Final Note

You have everything you need to make excellent progress. The limiting factor will not be your equipment — it will be consistency and patience. Three sessions per week, done intelligently for 12 weeks, will produce more visible and measurable change than most people achieve in a year of inconsistent training. Trust the process, follow the deloads, eat your protein, and sleep. The results will come.
